Assumptions and Conclusion

AreaAssumption / Interpretation
Data sourcePeloton Interactive, Inc. FY2025 Form 10-K, including comparative FY2024 and FY2023 operating and financial metrics.
Starting baseFY2025 ending paid Connected Fitness subscriptions of 2,799,943.
Default churnFY2025 Average Net Monthly Paid Connected Fitness Subscription Churn of 1.6%.
Default gross additions33,000 per month, a directional approximation calibrated to the FY2024–FY2025 net decline after applying FY2025 churn.
ARPUDefault $40.14/month, approximated from FY2025 subscription revenue divided by average paid Connected Fitness plus paid App subscriptions.
Risk logicRisk rises when the projected subscription base falls materially below FY2025 levels and annual subscription revenue / gross profit decline.
ConclusionPeloton is not currently in a death spiral because FY2025 adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow improved sharply and subscription margins remain high. However, the model flags real risk if churn continues rising and gross additions stay below monthly churn losses.
